In the morning, Lord Krishna, the Lord, full of compassion, shines with the radiance of a thousand suns illuminating the lotus flower. || 1 ||

Adorned with the beautiful sound of the flute, residing in the delightful city, beautiful grandson of the cowherds, granting happiness in Gokula, the auspicious one. || 2 ||

May the source of joy for Nanda, the wearer of the garland, the beautiful one bearing the flute, become the delight of Yashoda through morning remembrance. || 3 ||

O Raghava, with the moon-like face, lamp of the Yadu clan, if you have been awakened at dawn by Lord Krishna with loud sound... || 4 ||

Govinda, the protector of cows, bearer of the flute, holder of the flute, the connoisseur, delight of the Yadus, brother of Balarama, son of Devaki, Hari. || 5 ||

I bow to Him, the moon-faced one, marked with the Srivatsa symbol, adorned with the moon crest, accompanied by the flute-bearer. || 6 ||

Madhava, bearer of the flute, beloved of the cowherd women, at dawn, the ever-fresh one, Kesava, and Krishna indeed. || 7 ||

Beloved of Radha, king of rasa, lord of Gokula, Hari, at dawn worshipped by me with flower garlands, my Lord. || 8 ||

Son of Yashoda, excellent among the wise, husband of Revati, Lord of Kalindi, awakened from sleep, Krishna has come joyfully. || 9 ||

With face beautiful like a flute, maker of flute sounds, the wise one, at dawn filled with all virtues, may Madhava become manifest. || 10 ||
