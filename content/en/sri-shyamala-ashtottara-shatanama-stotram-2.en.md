## English Translation

Angya of this Sri Shyamala Ashtottara Shatanama Stotra Maha Mantra:
The Rishi is Mahabhairava,
The meter is Anushtup,
The deity is Sri Matangeshwari,
The seed is "Adishakti,"
The power (Shakti) is "Sarvakamapradaya,"
The kilaka is "Parajyoti Svarupini,"
The application is in the repetition of the Shyamala Ashtottara Shatanama.

Salutations to you, Oh mother of the world, Oh Matangeshwari, salutations to you,
To Shyamala, the lady of the world, salutations to you, Oh Supreme Goddess.

Salutations to you, Oh great Krishna (dark one), adorned with all ornaments,
Oh great goddess, Oh great ruler, Oh beloved of Mahadeva.

Adi Shakti, Maha Shakti, Para Shakti, Para Para,
Brahma Shakti, Vishnu Shakti, Shiva Shakti, salutations, salutations to you.

Salutations to Amrita-ishwari, Oh Goddess,
Salutations to you, Oh beloved of Parashiva,
In the form of Brahma, in the form of Vishnu, in the form of Shiva, salutations to you.

Granter of all desires, to you, salutations,
Granter of all accomplishments, to you, salutations,
Granter of all prosperity to men, Oh remover of all confusion.

Oh subduer of women, I bow to you,
Salutations to you, Oh subduer of men,
Oh bewilderer of gods, I serve you,
Oh subduer of all existences.

Salutations to Shankari, Oh goddess of speech,
Oh subduer of all worlds,
Oh grantor of all desired things, Oh eternal one,
Salutations to you, Oh Matangi's daughter.

Salutations to you who shine like a blue lotus,
Salutations to you whose radiance is like an emerald,
With the appearance of a blue cloud, Oh one whose radiance equals Indra's blue.

Salutations to you, Oh ruler of Chandis and other goddesses,
Oh divine subduer of women,
Salutations to you, Oh praised by mothers,
Oh Victory, Oh Victory, to you, salutations.

Adorned-limbed, Oh great Shyamala,
Oh great consort, Oh great radiance,
Oh dear to Mahavishnu, Oh very dear to Sadashiva.

Oh Rudrani, destroyer of all sins,
Oh Kameshwari, salutations to you,
Oh Shuka-Shyama (parrot-green), Oh Laghu-Shyama (light green),
Oh maker of kings submissive, salutations to you.

Oh holder of the vina, salutations to you always,
Salutations to you, Oh ever-delighted in music,
Granter of all knowledge, to you,
Salutations to you, Oh worshipped by Shakti and others.

I worship you, Oh sung in the Vedas,
Oh sung by gods, salutations, salutations to you,
Adorned with conch and earrings,
Oh Bimboshthi (lip-flower), I worship you.

Adorned with red garments at the hips,
Holding a honey pot,
Oh lover of honey, salutations to you,
Oh lover of the offering of flesh and honey.

Oh Ratakshi (red-eyed), Oh Ghanarnanakshi (cloud-eyed),
Oh smiling moon-faced, Oh praised one,
Adorned with kasturi tilaka,
Oh moon-crested, Oh one who is the world.

Salutations to you, Oh Mahalakshmi,
Abiding in the kadamba forest,
Salutations to you, Oh Mahavidya,
Oh whose breasts are heavy with their weight.

Praised by Hara, Hari and others,
Oh smiling-faced, I worship you,
Salutations to you, Oh bestower of auspiciousness to men,
Oh auspicious one, Oh abode of lotuses.

Oh destroyer of great poverty,
Oh burne r of great sins,
Oh bestower of great knowledge to men,
Salutations to you, Oh bestower of great beauty.

Oh bestower of great liberation, Oh Vani (speech),
Oh whose form is Supreme Light,
Oh whose nature is consciousness and bliss,
Salutations to you, Oh destroyer of Alakshmi (misfortune).

Oh bestower of fearlessness to devotees,
Oh destroyer of disease, salutations to you,
Salutations to you, Oh thousand-eyed,
Oh bearer of a thousand arms.

To me, Oh bestower of auspiciousness, to you,
Oh bestower of auspiciousness to devotees,
Salutations to you, Oh destroyer of inauspiciousness,
Oh bestower of eightfold prosperity, salutations to you.

Salutations to the Goddess, salutations to you,
Salutations to you, Oh necklace-maker,
Salutations to you, Oh mother of the world,
Salutations to you, Oh universal mistress.

Salutations to the high and low creeper,
Salutations to you, Oh beloved of the Supreme Self,
Salutations to you, Oh Rajamatangi,
Salutations to you, salutations to you.

**Phala Shruti (Fruits of Recitation) –**
The merit of these hundred and eight names of Shyamala described here,
Whoever recites with devotion is freed from all sins.

He becomes the great lord of Vagishvara (lord of speech) having mastered all scriptures,
Merely by listening once, he attains the four goals and the secret knowledge.

He subjugates all worlds with desire,
He becomes equal to Vishnu,
He obtains great Lakshmi (prosperity) difficult to obtain even for gods.

He obtains the eight powers (anima, etc.) quickly indeed,
He quickly becomes famous, he becomes the repository of all learning.

He attains supreme knowledge,
He always enjoys happiness,
He is worshipped everywhere,
He is victorious everywhere.

Fear of ghosts, spirits, and Pisachas does not arise for him,
He obtains great fame,
He attains the supreme yoga.

He becomes the master of the eight siddhis beginning with ghatika and paduka,
He is ever-auspicious, he becomes a great scholar.

He obtains long life,
He becomes a bewilderer of the world,
He obtains the great form of Mahadeva,
There is no doubt about this.
