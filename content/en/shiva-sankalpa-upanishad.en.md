## English Translation

(1) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that reality by which this entire world, past, present and future, is enveloped in immortality.

(2) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that by which the wise perform actions and toward which words and mind turn in pleasing ways.

(3) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which wise ascetics, intent on actions, hear in various sacrifices.

(4) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which is consciousness, will, and understanding, which is the inner light among beings, beyond which nothing is done.

(5) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which entered the heart like a well-controlled horse with good rider, adorned with auspicious garments.

(6) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that in which are the Rig verses, Sama hymns, Yajur formulas, in which stands the firm support of the world's extension.

(7) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which is the mysterious sixty-three-fold mighty power of the sacrifice, the wondrous illusion.

(8) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which, when awake, goes far, which when asleep goes as far, the distant journey of the lights, the one light.

(9) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that by which this universe became manifested, from which even the great gods were born; that same is fire, that is wind, that is sun, that is moon.

(10) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that by which heaven and earth and the atmosphere are held together, by which mountains, regions, and directions are upheld.

(11) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of those who are mind, heart, and gods, who are the divine waters, who are the rays of the sun; they move in ears and eyes.

(12) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that unthinkable, immeasurable, manifest and unmanifest, beyond, more subtle than the subtle.

(13-14) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the numbers from one to a hundred, to thousand, to lakh, to million, to billion; may the fires with their mind's offerings be propitiated.

(15) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that great person in the sun, beyond darkness, whose womb the wise behold.

(16) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which poets celebrate as the first-born, support of the immovable and mobile beings, the earth, atmosphere, and heaven.

(17) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that beyond the beyond, that beyond which there is nothing, that which is to be known as beyond the beyond.

(18) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the Brahman beyond the beyond, Hari beyond the beyond, that which rules beyond the beyond.

(19) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Gayatri, pervading all, the great goddess, in the Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva Vedas.

(20) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that great god whom the pure always worship as Pranava, and all the Vedas.

(21) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the Pranava syllable Om, the personification of sacrifice, the Purushottama, the Omkara, the self of Pranava.

(22) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that lord who is studied in all the Vedas, the bodiless, the attributeless, the Self.

(23) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that adorned with ghee, wealth, longevity, strength, offspring, and animals, the lotus-eyed one.

(24) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the three-eyed one, fragrant, increaser of prosperity, who frees us from bondage like a ripe cucumber from its stem, from death.

(25) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Kailasha peak, the beautiful abode of Shankara, where the gods rejoice.

(26) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the dweller on Kailasha peak, established on the snow mountain, the blue-throated one, the three-eyed one.

(27) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of him whose eyes, mouths, hands, and feet are everywhere, who embraces the earth and sky with his arms, who creates the gods as one.

(28) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of him who knows the four Vedas, master of all weapons, and the histories and Puranas.

(29) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Rudra; harm not our great ones, nor the young, nor the growing, nor the grown. Harm not our father, mother, or dear ones. Rudra, spare us from harm.

(30) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Rudra; harm not our men in homes, nor in life, nor in cows, nor in horses. Harm not our heroes. Rudra, who gives delight, harm us not. We worship you with obeisance.

(31) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the truth that is Rta, the supreme Brahman, the dark and red person, upward-rising, odd-eyed, universal-formed; salutations, salutations.

(32) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Rudra, red and blue, the most excellent lord of the prayers, whom we invoke for peace; he is Rudra; salutation to Rudra.

(33) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Brahman-knowledge placed first in the front, dear among the wise. His glory is the highest, he is established in truth and untruth.

(34) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which, winking quickly, became the sole king of the world. Who is the god to whom we should offer oblation?

(35) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that which gives strength and power, whose refuge the world takes, whose desire the gods obey, whose shadow is immortality and death is.

(36) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of Rudra who is in fire, in waters, in herbs, who has entered all worlds; salutation to Rudra.

(37) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the fragrant-doored, invincible, ever-nourishing, cruel one, the goddess of all beings, whom I invoke here.

(38) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of the namaka and chamaka hymns, the Purusha Sukta known to the wise, and the great god equivalent.

(39) May my mind have the auspicious resolve of that Shiva Sankalpa which Brahmins always meditate upon; they attain the supreme liberation.
