|| Shri Ganeshaya Namah ||

In the morning I remember Gananatha, the friend of the Lord,
Whose two tusks are resplendent with vermilion,
Who holds a formidable staff to break obstacles,
Who is worshipped by the multitude of gods led by Indra. || 1 ||

Arise, arise, O Ganesha! For the morning has come.
The deities are eager to worship you,
O Lord of obstacles, O Supreme Lord of gods. || 2 ||

With saffron-colored rays, bright and shining,
With water lilies blooming in streams,
May this morning bring auspiciousness quickly to your devotees, O Ganesha. || 3 ||

In the morning, I bow to you always, O Lord with elephant face,
O Supreme Lord. Salutations to you, the best guide,
Who grants fearlessness eternally. || 4 ||

May the morning shine with happiness, may Shambhu's son shine at dawn,
May this Ganesha shine in the morning, my beloved Gana. || 5 ||

Adorned in white garments, resplendent with the beauty of his face,
With a face like the moon, I take refuge in him,
Rising at dawn, I worship him. || 6 ||

He who eats modakas; without him, the living beings and Ganas,
Would not exist. O one worshipped even by Indra and others,
In the morning, I bow to you. || 7 ||

Salutation in the morning to the bestower of boons,
Whose feet cause the mouse to stumble,
O Gana-faced one. O Lord, grant me a boon. || 8 ||

You are born first eternally, your name shines first,
You are remembered first by all,
O Maheshvara, you are the first. || 9 ||

You are the one-tusked (Ekadanta), you are the second,
You are the three-eyed or four-formed one,
You are the five-faced, six-faced, or bearer of all forms, O Gana. || 10 ||

Elephant-faced, accompanied by elephants, or the Gana-worshipped,
Vinayaka, or the king of obstacles,
Ganapati, the bestower of boons. || 11 ||

Bestower of all accomplishments, destroyer of all obstacles,
Most auspicious among the auspicious, bestower of happiness to devotees. || 12 ||

In the morning I remember the Lord of gods, the Lord of obstacles,
Bestower of all accomplishments.
In the morning I bow to the Lord of gods, the elephant-faced one,
Like the Lord himself. || 13 ||

In the morning I worship the Lord of gods, the Lord of obstacles,
Bestower of all accomplishments.
In the morning I salute the Lord of gods, the elephant-faced one,
Like the Lord himself. || 14 ||
