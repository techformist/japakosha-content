prātaḥ smarāmi gaṇanāthamanāthabandhuṃ |
    sindūra-pūra-pariśobhitagaṇḍayugmam |
    uddaṃḍa-vighna-parikhaṇḍana-chanda-daṇḍaṃ |
    ākhaṇḍalādiśuranāyaka-bṛndavandyam || 1 ||

prātar-nāmāmi caturānana-vandyamānaṃ |
    icchā-anulamakhilaṃ varaṃ dadānāṃ |
    taṃ tuṇḍilam dvirasannādhipa yajña-sūtraṃ |
    putraṃ vilāsa-caturaṃ śivayoḥ śivāya || 2 ||

prātar-bhajāmy-abadhayaṃ khalu bhakta-śoka-
-dahanānalaṃ gaṇavibhuṃ vara-kunjarāsyam |
    ajñāna-kāma-vināśana-havavyavāhaṃ |
    utsāha-vardhanamaham sutaṃ īśvarasya || 3 ||

shloka-trayamidaM puNyaM sadaa saamraajya-daayakaM |
    praatar-utthaa-ya satataM yaH paThet-prayataH pumaaM || 4 ||

## English Translation

(1) In the morning I remember Gananatha, the friend of the Lord,
    Whose two tusks are resplendent with vermilion,
    Who holds a goad in His hand to break obstacles, a formidable staff,
    Who is worshipped by the multitude of leaders of the gods including Indra.

(2) In the morning I bow to the four-faced one who is worshipped,
    Who grants all desired boons,
    Him, the short one, the lord of the twice-born, the sacrificial cord,
    The son of Shiva, the playful one, auspicious to Shiva.

(3) In the morning I worship the fearless one, who is indeed the fire that destroys
    The grief of devotees, the Lord of ganas, with a face like an auspicious elephant,
    Who destroys the forest of ignorance, who increases enthusiasm,
    The noble son of the Lord of all.

(4) This three-verse hymn is sacred and always bestows sovereignty,
    Whosoever, rising at dawn, reads it with diligence, that man.
