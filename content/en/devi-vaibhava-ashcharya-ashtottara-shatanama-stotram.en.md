## English Translation

Meditation |
    She whose radiance equals the brightness of kumkum,
    Who holds goad, noose, trident, and spear in hand,
    Who stands amidst the lotus and has eyes like lotus petals,
    To that Supreme Goddess I bow.
    | Five-fold Worship |
    To Her who is of the nature of earth, I offer sandalwood,
    To Her who is of the nature of space, I worship with flowers,
    To Her who is of the nature of air, I offer incense,
    To Her who is of the nature of fire, I show the lamp,
    To Her who is of the nature of nectar, I offer the great nectar-oblation,
    To Her who is of the nature of all, I offer all services.
    Om Aim Hrim Shrim |
    She who is a wave of supreme bliss, a lamp of higher consciousness,
    She who is self-effulgent light, ever-abounding in splendor. || 1 ||

She who is pure, the sole reality, the very form of time and truth,
    She who is without beginning, middle, or end, who sports in great maya. || 2 ||

She who is the beholder of the three gunas, the illuminer of all principles,
    She who delights in feminine and masculine natures, beautified by creation of the world. || 3 ||

She whose infinite names and forms shine like the sun,
    She who is the form of primordial tendencies, awakening the universe. || 4 ||

She who is the queller of the universe, consisting of moving and unmoving beings,
    She who is the support of the entire world, interested in the life of all. || 5 ||

She who is the very mind of devotees, endless, playing in Her own splendor,
    She who attracts all, controlling all actions, bearing all burdens. || 6 ||

She who is knowledge, supreme bliss, the science that grants progeny and perfection,
    She who grants longevity, health, auspiciousness, strength, fame, and good fortune. || 7 ||

She who bestows wealth, grains, gems, clothes, ornaments, unguents, and garlands,
    She who grants the happiness of household, village, kingdom, and empire. || 8 ||

She who is complete with the seven powers, bestowing all worldly fruits,
    She who is worshiped by Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Indra, and other gods. || 9 ||

She who grants enjoyment, liberation, great devotion, detachment, and non-duality,
    She who presides over control and grace, granting knowledge and non-duality. || 10 ||

She who grants yogic powers such as entering another's body,
    She who protects the good, destroys the wicked, and grants perfection. || 11 ||

She who has playfully created millions of cosmic spheres,
    She who is one and many, assuming many forms, the Goddess of the half-lord. || 12 ||

She who is composed of Shiva and Shakti, ever-delighting in amorous union,
    She who is satisfied, nourished, endless, ever-youthful and enchanting. || 13 ||

She who assumes the form of all goddesses, the goddess of all gods,
    She who is embodied in the gods, sages, ancestors, accomplished ones, yoginis, and Bhairavas. || 14 ||

She who is the treasure, perfection, gem, and seal,
    She who is armed with weapons, bearing flags and adorned with umbrellas and chamaras. || 15 ||

She who is served by elephants, horses, chariots, foot-soldiers, ministers, and armies,
    She who is worshipped by priests, teachers, gurus, and disciples. || 16 ||

She who dwells in the midst of the nectar ocean, in the divine tree,
    She who dwells in the kadamba forest, amidst the jewel islands. || 17 ||

She who dwells within the Chintamani house, in the center of the gem pavilion,
    She who reigns on the jeweled throne, seated on the divine couch. || 18 ||

She who is united at the base of the great Shiva linga,
    She whose mind is agitated by the mutual friction of the lingas. || 19 ||

She who is in the series of bindus and kala, beyond the beyond,
    She who is the nectar of speech, self-manifest, the bliss at the end of sound. || 20 ||

She who is skilled in the secrets of Kamaraja's great tantra,
    She who is beautiful, delighting in the five Makaras. || 21 ||

She who dwells in the king of Sri Chakra, the embodiment of Sri Vidya mantra,
    She who is the form of the undifferentiated existence-consciousness-bliss, the union of Shiva and Shakti. || 22 ||

She who is Tripura, the Lady of Tripura, the Great Lady of Tripura,
    She who delights in dwelling in Tripura, whose very form is Sri Tripura. || 23 ||

She who dwells at the end of the great lotus forest, the beautiful Tripura Malini,
    She who is the Great Lady of Tripura, the Mother of Tripura. || 24 ||

She who is the Goddess of the nine chakras, the Great Bhairavi of Tripura,
    She who is the Divine Mother, Lalita, Bala, Rajarajeshwari, and Shiva. || 25 ||

She who dwells in the center of the wheel of creation, sustentation, and dissolution,
    She who abides in the half of Meru as the very self, the Goddess of all worlds. || 26 ||

She who dwells in the center of Valmiki's city, in the Jambu forest,
    She who dwells on the peak of Arunachala, in the tiger's cave. || 27 ||

She who dwells in Srikalahasti, in the city of Kashi,
    She who dwells on Mount Kailasa, the Goddess of the twelve endings. || 28 ||

She who is established in the center of the sixteen, witnessed by all Vedanta,
    She who is the Goddess of Shruti, Smriti, Puranas, history, and Agamas. || 29 ||

She who consists of the five elements, the senses, the deities, the vital breaths, and the heart,
    She who is the form of the individual soul, the Supreme Soul, and Brahma, the abode of virtues. || 30 ||

She who is free from the three states, who consists of speech and immeasurable glory,
    She who assumes the forms of Gayatri, Bhuvaneshwari, Durga, Kali, and others. || 31 ||

She who sports in various forms like Fish, Tortoise, and Boar,
    She who is worshipped by the Great Lord of Yogis, granting the boon of great valor. || 32 ||

She who is worshipped by the host of Siddheshvaras, whose feet are of great splendor. || 33 ||

Meditation again –
    She whose radiance equals the brightness of kumkum,
    Who holds goad, noose, trident, and spear in hand,
    Who stands amidst the lotus and has eyes like lotus petals,
    To that Supreme Goddess I bow.
